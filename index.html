Gothic Cathedrals

Gothic cathedrals are religious buildings characterized by pointed arches, flying buttresses, ribbed vaults, and stained glass windows, built in Europe between the mid-12th and early 16th centuries. They were a prominent feature of the Gothic architectural style. These cathedrals often took centuries to complete, showcasing the combined efforts of master architects, carpenters, stonemasons, and glassworkers.
